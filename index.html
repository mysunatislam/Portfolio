PDAC Biomarker Discovery & Biological Network Analysis

Problem: pancreatic ductal adenocarcinoma is often detected late, and early non-invasive biomarkers remain unreliable.

What I built

An integrated workflow combining transcriptomics, spatial validation, protein-interaction networks, enrichment analysis, and extracellular-vesicle miRNA validation.

Quantified impact

  • Integrated 5 datasets covering 428 samples/regions.
  • Reduced 628 malignant-switch genes to 80 spatially validated genes.
  • Identified 13 progression genes and 18 hub genes linked to PDAC biology.
  • Built a 6 EV-miRNA biomarker panel with ROC AUC 0.935 and 10-fold CV AUC 0.919.
  • Secondary validation detected 12 of 17 candidates and confirmed 5 directionally concordant biomarkers.

Microscopy Phenotypic Analysis Pipeline

Problem: fluorescence microscopy drug screens need consistent normalization, reliable segmentation, and quantitative per-cell outputs.

What I built

A hybrid classical and deep-learning image-analysis workflow for DAPI, Actin, and Tubulin channels.

Quantified impact

  • Built drug-effect outputs including dose-response curves, phenotype heatmaps, and PCA separation maps.

Neuromechanical Reflex Modeling for Robotic Hands

Problem: soft robotic hands lack fast, biomimetic reflex behavior that can stabilize motion and reduce unsafe force under disturbance.

What I built

A simulation framework for human-inspired reflex control across stretch reflex, Golgi-like inhibition, event-triggered withdrawal, and descending gain modulation.

Quantified impact

  • Modeled 4 control modes for protective and adaptive actuator behavior.
  • Targeted biological reflex response windows of 20-50 ms for rapid disturbance handling.
  • Simulated a 0.85 load step where inhibitory gain dropped from 1.00 to ~0.78.
  • Verified event-triggered withdrawal at ~1 second with smooth non-oscillatory decay.

Engineering impact

Gives rehab robotics teams a simulation-first way to evaluate protective reflex behavior before physical actuator testing.

Nanoparticle Drug Delivery Optimization

Problem: nanoparticle carrier selection needs statistically defensible comparison across release rate, carrier type, and experimental conditions.

What I built

A validation framework for comparing nanoparticle delivery performance using experimental design, hypothesis testing, and post-hoc analysis.

Quantified impact

  • Compared 3 nanoparticle/carrier groups: Au-based, liposomal, and polymer-based systems.
  • Used a 3x3 full-factorial DOE with ANOVA and Tukey post-hoc testing at alpha=0.05.
  • Polymer-based delivery averaged 60.8% release over 6 hours versus 51.7% for Au-based delivery.
  • Result: +9.2 percentage points absolute lift and ~17.7% relative improvement in average release.

Evidence

Determination of Efficiency Among Nano-Particles and Carriers for Drug Delivery
